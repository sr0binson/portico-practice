Troubleshooting Reference
Learn the framework first. Every Portico ticket follows the same logic. Click each module to expand.
The universal first move: Ask yourself -- what system is involved? Then: is it a user error, a configuration issue, a sync problem, or a bug? Start there before you do anything else.
How Portico's systems connect: Verity IQ (CRM/SIS) is the core. Campus Ivy (FA) pulls student records from it. CourseKey (Attendance/Payments) syncs with SIS. Trajecsys (Clinical) is separate but connected. If something breaks, it often breaks at a sync point between two systems.

The 5-Step Framework for Any Ticket

Use this on every ticket regardless of module

⌄
Step 1
Identify the system
Which Portico module is involved? CRM (Verity Dialog), SIS (Verity Student), FA (Campus Ivy Core), Docs (Verity Paperless Campus), Attendance/Skills (CourseKey), Interventions (Smart Nudges), or Clinical (Trajecsys)?
Step 2
Identify who is affected
Is this one user, one campus, one role, or all users? One person = likely a permissions/login issue or user error. Everyone = likely a sync failure, bug, or outage.
Step 3
Ask the clarifying questions
What exactly did they try to do? What happened instead? What did the error message say (exact text or code)? Has this happened before? Did anything change recently?
Step 4
Try the basic fixes first
Log out and back in. Clear cache/cookies. Try a different browser. Confirm their role/permissions are correct in User Management. Check if data is missing in the upstream system (usually Verity Student for SIS issues).
Step 5
Document and escalate if needed
If basic steps don't fix it: document what you tried, what the error was, which system, how many users affected, and whether it's SLA-sensitive. Escalate to Engineering with full context -- never escalate a half-described ticket.
Verity Dialog

CRM -- Verity Dialog

Admissions, leads, enrollment pipeline, outreach

⌄
What this module does
Tracks prospective students from first inquiry through enrollment. Used mostly by admissions staff. Shows lead status, contact history, enrollment stage, and team performance metrics.
Common issues you'll see
  • Lead status not updating after a call or email
  • Student showing up twice (duplicate record)
  • Admissions rep can't see their assigned leads
  • Analytics data looks wrong or not refreshing
  • New inquiry form not sending to the CRM
How to troubleshoot
Lead not updating: Confirm the rep saved the activity log. Check if their role has edit permissions. Try refreshing after a few minutes -- some updates are near-real-time not instant.

Duplicate record: This is usually a data entry issue. Confirm which is the correct record (most recent activity). Flag for admin to merge -- don't delete.

Can't see leads: Check their user role and territory/assignment settings. This is almost always a permissions issue.

Analytics wrong: Confirm the date range filter. Some reports pull from Verity Student (SIS) -- if data is missing there, it won't show here.
Error codes for this module
401Session expired or wrong permissions. Have them log out and back in. If it persists, check their role in User Management.
404Record not found. The lead may have been deleted or they're searching with the wrong ID.
422Form validation error. A required field is missing or formatted wrong. Walk them through the required fields.
Verity Student

SIS -- Verity Student

Student records, enrollment, grades, transcripts

⌄
What this module does
The central student database. Registrars use it. Stores enrollment status, grades, clock hours, academic records, and program information. Other modules (FA, CourseKey) pull from here -- if Verity Student has bad data, everything downstream will too.
Common issues you'll see
  • Student record missing or showing wrong program
  • Grade or hours not reflecting correctly
  • Transcript won't generate
  • Enrollment status wrong (active vs. withdrawn)
  • Student doesn't appear in CourseKey or Campus Ivy
How to troubleshoot
Student missing in another module: Always check Verity Student first. If they're not active and correctly enrolled here, they won't appear anywhere else. This is the source of truth.

Grade/hours wrong: Confirm the data was entered (not just saved in draft). Check if CourseKey sync has occurred -- attendance hours flow from CourseKey into SIS.

Transcript won't generate: Usually missing required data (incomplete record, missing dates, or SAP hold). Ask what error appears. Check for a financial hold in Campus Ivy -- FA holds can block transcripts.
Error codes for this module
500Server error on their end. Document it, check if others are affected. If widespread, escalate to Engineering as a potential outage.
422Data validation failed. A required field is missing in the student record -- usually program, start date, or enrollment status.
Sync errorCourseKey data didn't push to SIS. Check if the sync job ran. If not, it may need to be triggered manually by Engineering.
Campus Ivy

Financial Aid -- Campus Ivy Core

FAFSA, Title IV, disbursements, SAP, COD

⌄
What this module does
Manages all federal financial aid for students. FA Directors use it. Handles FAFSA processing, aid packaging, disbursements, Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) checks, and reporting to COD (Common Origination and Disbursement) and NSLDS.
Why FA tickets are high priority
Financial aid issues directly affect students' ability to stay enrolled. A disbursement error or SAP hold can mean a student can't pay tuition. These are almost always urgent -- treat them as high priority and loop in the FA Director if you're unsure.
Common issues you'll see
  • Student aid package not showing or calculating wrong
  • Disbursement not processing
  • SAP hold incorrectly applied
  • ISIR data not importing from FAFSA
  • COD or NSLDS reporting error
  • Workflow item stuck (red column in Campus Ivy dashboard)
How to troubleshoot
Aid not showing: Check if the student's ISIR was imported. If no ISIR = no aid package. Also confirm enrollment status in Verity Student -- aid requires active enrollment.

Disbursement not processing: Check the workflow dashboard for red/stuck items. Check for a SAP hold or enrollment status issue. Disbursements also require the correct credit balance -- confirm with FA Director.

ISIR not importing: This is usually a COD/federal connection issue. Document the student ID, what was submitted, and escalate to Engineering -- don't try to manually fix federal data.

SAP hold wrong: Confirm the student's actual hours and GPA meet the program's SAP requirements. If the hold looks like an error, flag for FA Director to review -- never manually remove an SAP hold without approval.
Key terms to know
ISIRInstitutional Student Information Record -- the processed FAFSA data. Must be imported before aid can be packaged.
SAPSatisfactory Academic Progress. Students must meet GPA/completion rate thresholds to keep aid. A hold here blocks disbursement.
CODCommon Origination and Disbursement -- the federal system schools report disbursements to. Errors here are serious.
R2T4Return to Title IV. When a student withdraws, the school may owe money back to the federal government. Campus Ivy calculates this.
Title IVFederal student aid programs (Pell, subsidized/unsubsidized loans). Trade schools are heavily dependent on this.
CourseKey

Attendance & Skills -- CourseKey

Clock hours, attendance, skills checklists, payments

⌄
What this module does
Tracks student attendance by clock hours (not credits -- trade schools work in hours). Also tracks skills completion via checklists, handles payment processing, and feeds attendance data into Verity Student (SIS).
Common issues you'll see
  • Attendance hours not syncing to Verity Student
  • Student hours showing wrong total
  • Skills checklist won't save or submit
  • Payment not processing or showing wrong balance
  • Student can't clock in (mobile app issue)
How to troubleshoot
Hours not syncing: This is the most common CourseKey issue. The sync between CourseKey and Verity Student runs on a schedule. Ask when it was last correct. If it's been more than a few hours, escalate as a sync failure.

Wrong hours total: Check if any hours were entered manually vs. system-tracked. Also check the Sync/Async tabs -- Async hours are often reviewed separately.

Student can't clock in: Check if they're enrolled in the correct program in CourseKey. Also check device/app permissions. Sometimes it's a mobile app update issue.
Error codes for this module
Webhook timeoutThe sync from CourseKey to Verity Student failed mid-transfer. Hours may be stuck. Escalate to Engineering to re-trigger the webhook.
422Skills checklist submission failed -- usually a required field is blank or the student record is missing data in SIS.
Trajecsys

Clinical Management -- Trajecsys

Externships, preceptors, clinical hours, nursing programs

⌄
What this module does
Manages clinical rotations and externships for nursing and allied health programs. Tracks hours at clinical sites, preceptor assignments, skills performed in the field, and compliance documentation. Acquired by Portico late 2025.
Common issues you'll see
  • Clinical hours not showing in student record
  • Preceptor not assigned or can't access the student's file
  • Compliance documents missing or not accepted
  • Clinical coordinator can't see a student's site assignment
  • NCLEX prep tracking not updating
How to troubleshoot
Clinical hours not showing: Confirm the preceptor logged the hours (they enter them, not the student). Check that the student is assigned to the correct clinical site in Trajecsys.

Preceptor access issue: Preceptors are external users -- their accounts are often set up differently. Confirm their login was created and they have the correct site access.

This module is newer to Portico -- if you're unsure, it's okay to say you'll escalate to Engineering for anything beyond basic access issues.
Escalation

When and How to Escalate

What goes to Engineering vs. what you handle

⌄
Escalate immediately if:
  • A 500 error is happening for multiple users at the same school
  • A disbursement or financial aid action is blocked and time-sensitive
  • A sync/webhook failure means attendance hours are not reaching SIS
  • A student's federal data (COD, NSLDS, ISIR) looks wrong
  • An SLA breach is 90% or higher on a high-priority ticket
Handle yourself first if:
  • It's a login issue (most are 401 -- try logout/login, check role)
  • It's a "where is my record" question (search with correct ID)
  • It's a user who doesn't know how to use a feature (walkthrough)
  • It's a data entry mistake the admin can correct themselves
What to include in an escalation
Ticket ID, school name, affected user(s), module, exact error message or code, steps already tried, number of users affected, SLA status, and whether it's affecting live student financial aid or enrollment.
Ticket Practice
Pick a ticket. Work through it step by step. You'll get feedback at each step.
Interview Drill
Read the question. Think of your answer. Then reveal the suggested response and what to avoid.
J
Joshua
Senior Support Analyst -- will test your technical knowledge and support process
Walk me through how you'd handle a ticket where a school says their student's financial aid isn't showing up.
Why they ask: Tests your FA knowledge and whether you know the right order of operations.
First I'd confirm which module they're in -- Campus Ivy Core. Then I'd check if the student's ISIR was imported, because without that, aid can't be packaged at all. I'd also verify the student is active and correctly enrolled in Verity Student, since Campus Ivy pulls from there. If both look correct, I'd check the Campus Ivy workflow dashboard for any stuck or red items. If something's blocked and it's affecting a disbursement, I'd escalate to Engineering with the full context and flag it as time-sensitive.
Avoid: Jumping to "I'd escalate it" without showing the diagnostic steps first. Joshua wants to see that you don't just pass things off.
A school calls and says their attendance hours aren't syncing to student records. What do you do?
Why they ask: Tests CourseKey + SIS integration knowledge -- this is a very common Portico issue.
I'd confirm they're looking at CourseKey for attendance and Verity Student for the record -- those are two different systems and the sync runs on a schedule. I'd ask when they last saw it working correctly. If it's been more than a few hours since a sync should have run, that's likely a webhook failure between CourseKey and SIS. I'd document the details -- school name, how many students affected, time range -- and escalate to Engineering to re-trigger the sync job. I'd follow up with the school once I have an ETA from the team.
Avoid: Telling them to just wait or refresh. That's not enough for a sync failure.
What's the difference between a 401 error and a 500 error, and how would you handle each?
Why they ask: Basic technical competency check -- every support role needs this.
A 401 is an authorization error -- the user doesn't have permission or their session expired. I'd start with logout and login, then check their role and permissions in User Management. A 500 is a server-side error that's not caused by the user -- something broke on the backend. One person getting a 500 might be a fluke, but if multiple people at the same school are seeing it, that's a potential outage and I'd escalate to Engineering immediately with as much detail as I have.
Avoid: Saying both errors mean "something is broken." The distinction matters.
Tell me about a time you had to figure something out on your own with limited documentation.
Why they ask: Portico is a newer, merged platform. You will hit undocumented situations. They want to know you won't freeze.
When I built the Portico Support Dashboard for my portfolio, I was working with the Airtable API and Make.com to build escalation logic from scratch -- there wasn't a guide for exactly what I was trying to do. I had to read the API docs, test small pieces, and work backward from the errors I got. That approach -- isolate the variable, test one thing at a time, read the error carefully -- is how I'd work through a support issue with limited documentation too.
Avoid: A vague story with no specifics. Name the actual thing you figured out and how.
How would you prioritize a queue with five open tickets when two of them are about to breach SLA?
Why they ask: SLA management is a core part of this role.
I'd address the two SLA-breaching tickets first, starting with whichever has a higher impact -- for example, one involving financial aid or active student access would jump ahead of a reporting question. For the SLA tickets, my first goal is acknowledgment and triage, not full resolution -- even sending a quality first response buys time and keeps the customer informed. Then I'd work through the remaining three by priority and complexity, combining any that are the same issue if possible.
Avoid: Just saying "first in first out." That's not how SLA-aware support works.
T
Tina
Customer Success Manager -- will test your communication, empathy, and client relationship skills
How do you communicate bad news to a client -- like when a fix is going to take longer than expected?
Why she asks: Tina cares about the client relationship. She wants to know you won't go silent or overpromise.
I'd reach out proactively -- before they have to follow up. I'd tell them what I know, what's still being worked on, and give a realistic timeframe if I have one. If I don't have a timeline yet, I'd say that clearly rather than guessing. I'd also tell them what I'm doing in the meantime and when they can expect another update. The goal is that they never wonder if their issue was forgotten.
Avoid: "I'd escalate and let them know when it's fixed." That's passive. Tina wants to hear that you stay in contact.
Tell me about a time you turned a frustrated customer into a satisfied one.
Why she asks: This is a classic CS question. She wants empathy AND a real example.
At Trader Joe's I was promoted to Mate within 6 weeks, which meant I was handling customer complaints and difficult situations on the floor every shift. There was a customer who came in upset about a product issue -- I didn't argue with her, I just listened first and made sure she felt heard before I did anything. Then I solved the actual problem quickly. She came back later that day to tell my captain I was the reason she'd keep shopping with us. I think the instinct to listen before fixing has carried into everything I've done since.
Avoid: A vague story or one where the customer "ended up okay." Be specific and show it was your actions that changed it.
What do you know about Portico and why do you want to work here specifically?
Why she asks: She's testing whether you did your homework and whether you're actually interested or just job hunting.
Portico launched in May 2025 by merging four companies -- Verity IQ, Campus Ivy, CourseKey, and Trajecsys -- into one platform for career-focused schools. You serve over 300 institutions and 400,000 students in trade, nursing, allied health, and beauty programs. What stood out to me is that this isn't generic EdTech -- it's built for students who are trying to get into a career, not a four-year degree. That matters. I also built a support dashboard modeled after your actual workflow to prepare for this role, which gave me hands-on experience with SLA tracking, escalation logic, and API integration in a Portico-style context. I want to be here because the work is real and the impact on students is direct.
Avoid: "I saw it on LinkedIn and it seemed like a good fit." That's a non-answer. Show you researched them.
How do you handle a situation where a client is demanding something you can't actually give them?
Why she asks: This happens constantly in B2B SaaS support. She wants to know you can hold a boundary without losing the relationship.
I'd acknowledge what they're asking for and why it makes sense from their perspective. Then I'd be honest about what I can and can't do -- I wouldn't promise something just to get off the call. If there's a workaround or an alternative that gets them most of what they need, I'd offer that. If it's a feature request, I'd let them know I'll pass it to the Product team so it's on record. The goal is for them to feel like their frustration was heard and their ask was taken seriously, even if the answer is no right now.
Avoid: "I'd just escalate it." That's not managing the client relationship. Tina wants to see you can handle the conversation yourself.
What do you think makes someone successful in a role like this at a company like Portico?
Why she asks: She's looking for self-awareness and cultural fit. Portico values: customer-centricity, innovation, transparency. Thrives: visionaries, team players, self-starters.
Curiosity and follow-through. In a platform that's still being unified from four different systems, there will always be something you don't know yet -- so you have to be willing to dig, learn fast, and ask good questions. But curiosity without follow-through just creates noise. The schools you support are dealing with real student outcomes, so they need someone who says "I'll get back to you by end of day" and actually does. I also think transparency matters -- being honest about what you don't know builds more trust than pretending.
Avoid: "I'm a hard worker and a fast learner." That's generic. Tie it to something specific about Portico.
Wildcard Prep
Unexpected things that might come up. Be ready for these.
These are curveballs -- questions or moments that can catch you off guard if you haven't thought about them. Read each one and think through your answer before the interview.
Role Play
"Let's do a quick role play. I'm a school administrator and I can't log in to the system. Go."
How to handle it: Don't panic. Treat it like a real ticket. Start with "Thanks for reaching out -- let's get this sorted. Can you tell me what error you're seeing when you try to log in?" Then work through it: 401 = permissions/session. No error = possibly wrong URL or browser issue. Account locked = check with their admin. Stay calm, be methodical, don't rush to a solution before you have the info.
Silence
"What questions do you have for us?" (and you go blank)
Backup questions to always have ready: "What does a successful first 90 days look like in this role?" / "What's the most common type of ticket your team handles right now?" / "How does the support team collaborate with Engineering when something needs to be escalated?" / "How is Portico approaching the integration of the four platforms over the next year?" Any of these show you're thinking about the job, not just the offer.
Weakness Question
"What's a weakness you're actively working on?"
How to handle it: Don't say "I work too hard" or "I'm a perfectionist." Be real. A strong answer: "I'm still building my depth on enterprise SaaS platforms -- I've been studying Portico's platform specifically and built a simulator to practice, but I know there's a learning curve and I'm committed to getting up to speed fast." That turns a gap into evidence of your growth mindset.
Experience Gap
"You don't have direct SaaS support experience. Why should we hire you?"
How to handle it: Don't apologize for your resume. "My IT internship gave me real troubleshooting experience across 12 school sites and Zendesk. I built a support dashboard modeled after Portico's workflow. I've been studying your platform for weeks. I know I'm not coming in with years of SaaS experience, but I'm coming in with the instincts, the prep, and the drive -- and I don't think you'll find someone who's done more to prepare for this specific role."
Compensation
"Are you okay with the $58k salary range?"
How to handle it: The listed salary is $58k. If you're comfortable with that, a simple "Yes, that works for me" is fine. If you want to negotiate later, now is not the time -- lock in the offer first. You can say "I'm focused on finding the right role and company -- I'm confident we can make it work if there's a mutual fit."
The Simulator
If they ask you to show your portfolio or walk them through your work
How to handle it: Lead with the Portico Support Dashboard at portico-dashboard.vercel.app. Explain: "I built this to model how support operations work in a SaaS company. It pulls live ticket data from Airtable, tracks SLA status, and uses a Make.com automation to auto-escalate overdue tickets every 15 minutes." Then pivot: "I also built a Portico platform simulator to learn your modules before this interview." That's a strong one-two punch.
Culture Fit
"How would you describe your work style?"
How to handle it: Tie it to Portico's values. "I'm a self-starter -- I don't wait for someone to tell me what to learn. I built prep tools for this interview on my own. I'm also a team player: I know support doesn't work in isolation and I'd want to be useful to Engineering and Customer Success, not just the people filing tickets. And I'm customer-first -- at Trader Joe's that was the whole job, and I carried it into my IT work too."
